Universal Design

Task 3.3 – Principles of universal design and homes for smart ageing Think about a friend or family member who is older. List the top 10 ways that universal design can help them live a longer and happier life.

10. Better sinks Sinks with knobs could be a bad thing to have when elders need to clean their hands. Elders get weaker, and it can be difficult for them to turn the knob. Therefore, it can be better to have sinks with levers, or even better, sinks with sensors since it easier for them to clean their hands.

9. Safer stairs Stairs should be implemented with handrails so people can safely go up or down the stairs without having the risk of falling. Good lighting is also a must for better view. A stair lift is also possible to implement for those who are disabled.

8. Better baths Baths or preferably showers should have a seat and handrail so it could be easier and safer for the elders to clean themselves without having them slipping while doing their business.

7. Better counters Counters can be more versatile if the height is adjustable. This makes it so that the elders don’t have to reach far.

6. Better doors Doors and door arches need to be wider, and doors should have a door handle so the elders can enter in an easier way with a cane or wheelchair.

5. Better cabinets Like the counters, these cabinets should also be adjustable so that even people in wheelchairs can use them easily.

4. Slip resistant floors As listed before, elders are vulnerable and can fall anywhere so it’s important to have a slip resistant floor so any elder can’t slip.

3. Emergency button As people get older, they also get more vulnerable and can therefore get into many risks of damaging themselves. There should be an emergency button that is easily accessible so they can get help more efficiently if they for example get a heart attack or slip. This can save their lives.

2. Smart assistant By implementing a smart assistant like Google Home or Amazons Alexa, elders can learn to use it to do simple tasks like turning on the lights, start the TV or even play music, resulting in a happier life and not only that but can be a great way to call emergency if in need.

1. No step entry home With a no step home it can not only be easier for disabled people to traverse their home but by not having any stairs it becomes more safer since every year there is an average of 12.000 deaths as a result of stairway accidents (Amstep Products, 2020).
